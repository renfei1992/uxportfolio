Axxess Mobile App for Freelancing Nurses

My Role

In Summer 2016, I joined Axxess as a user experience design intern. I helped Axxess design new products as well as redesign flagship products to shape its future. When I started at Axxess, Axxess was working on a new mobile product to bridge the gap between patients and freelance nurses. My responsibility was to design user profile UI for nurses to manage their personal information.

Start with User Tasks

While having a great level of freedom to create these pages, I need to keep these user tasks in mind so that the profile pages have all the features we need:

1. See and Edit Profile Information

2. View Daily and Weekly Earning Report

3. View Ratings and Detailed Reviews

4. Keep Motivated to Keep Using and Performing Well

After realizing the requirements for the design, I quickly sketched out some layouts. To fully optimize the experience on a native app and facilitate one-hand option, I made the four user tasks above into four tabs on the bottom navigation.

Who's the Boss? Users

Who can tell me which design works better for users? It has to be the actual users - the nurses. So I should stop making assumptions and start asking for feedback from the people who I was designing for. I conducted a mini A/B test with nurses to learn which options were more intuitive. Here are some learnings from the mini A/B test:

High-Fidelity UI

Profile

User can see and edit their profile information as well as change their profile photos with a tap on the button.

Earnings

User can see their weekly earnings chart on the upper section and transactions sorted by date on the lower section.

Ratings

Users can see their overall ratings on the upper section and view the detailed reviews on the lower section.

Badges

I designed a little gamification (achievement system) to encourage users to maintain good performance.

Onboarding Screens

Other than the user profile, our new application has many exciting features we couldn't wait to share with our lovely users. Therefore, I worked with both the marketing team and mobile development team to understand what new features are most important and what the best ways are to present the features with "Axxess personality".

After discussing with multiple stakeholders, I designed the splash onboarding screens to deliver a graphical illustration of our new features. We tested the design with our technical team and QA team to refine the information architecture and visual design.
